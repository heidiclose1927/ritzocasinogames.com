RitzocasinoGames.com is dedicated to:

  • Preventing Underage Gaming: We strictly prohibit anyone under the legal gambling age from accessing and participating in our games.

Underage gambling is illegal and irresponsible. RitzocasinoGames.com takes stringent measures to prevent minors from accessing our platform:

  • Awareness: We strive to raise awareness among our players about the importance of keeping their account details secure and logging out after each session to prevent unauthorized use by minors.

3. Preventing Compulsive Gambling and Providing Support

While gambling can be entertaining, for some individuals, it can become a problem. We offer various tools and resources to promote responsible play:

  • Self-Exclusion: Players can choose to temporarily or permanently exclude themselves from playing on RitzocasinoGames.com. During a self-exclusion period, you will not be able to access your account, deposit funds, or place bets. To initiate self-exclusion, please contact our support team.
  • Deposit Limits: We provide options for players to set daily, weekly, or monthly deposit limits. Once set, you will not be able to deposit more than your chosen limit within the specified timeframe.
  • Session Limits: Players can set limits on the duration of their gaming sessions. A notification will appear when your session limit is reached, prompting you to log out or take a break.
  • Reality Checks: We may implement features that provide periodic reminders of the time spent playing and the amounts wagered, encouraging players to be mindful of their activity.
  • Financial Awareness: We encourage players to gamble only with money they can afford to lose and never to gamble with funds intended for essential living expenses.

4. Recognizing Problem Gambling

It's important to recognize the signs of problem gambling, both in yourself and in others. Some common indicators include:

  • Spending more money and time on gambling than you can afford.
  • Feeling the need to be secretive about your gambling.
  • Having difficulty stopping or controlling your gambling.
  • Gambling to escape from problems or feelings of anxiety, guilt, or depression.
  • Chasing losses (trying to win back money you've lost).
  • Neglecting responsibilities at work, school, or home due to gambling.
  • Borrowing money or selling possessions to fund gambling.

If you or someone you know exhibits these signs, we strongly recommend seeking help.

5. Seeking Help and Support

If you feel your gambling is becoming a problem, or if you are concerned about a friend or family member, please consider reaching out to professional help organizations. Many organizations offer confidential advice and support. Some widely recognized resources include:

  • Gamblers Anonymous: A fellowship of men and women who share their experience, strength, and hope with each other that they may solve their common problem and help others to recover from a gambling problem.
  • National Council on Problem Gambling (NCPG): Offers a national helpline and resources for help and hope.
  • GamCare (UK): Provides support, information, and advice to anyone experiencing problems with gambling.
  • Other Local Support Services: Many countries and regions have local organizations dedicated to helping individuals with gambling problems. We encourage you to search for resources specific to your location.
